Key Types of Machine Learning

ML models learn in different ways depending on the data they're given and the problem they need to solve. This section explains the two primary approaches. Click the ‚ú® button for an AI-powered simple explanation!

Supervised Learning

The algorithm learns from data that is already **labeled** with the correct outcome. It's like studying for an exam with an answer key. The goal is to predict a future outcome based on past examples.

Classification

Predicting a category.

  • Is this insurance claim fraudulent or not?
  • Will this customer renew their subscription?
Regression

Predicting a continuous value.

  • What will our revenue be next quarter?
  • What is the probable lifetime value of this customer?

Unsupervised Learning

The algorithm explores **unlabeled** data to find hidden structures, patterns, or groupings on its own. The goal is to discover underlying patterns without a predefined target.

Clustering

Finding natural groupings.

  • Group customers into distinct segments.
  • Identify different types of network traffic.
Association

Discovering "if-then" rules.

  • Find products that are frequently bought together.
  • Identify which services are used in sequence.
